Core Claim

There is no view from nowhere.

Reality is what survives consistency across observer patches.

OPH starts from local observers on a holographic screen, not a universe with a built-in god's-eye frame. Where patches overlap, their descriptions must agree. The laws of physics are the constraints that make that agreement possible.

If you come from simulation theory or reality-as-simulation debates, OPH gives that intuition a more formal target: reality behaves like an observer-consistent information process, not a passive stage with one privileged viewpoint.

Coverage

What OPH is trying to connect.

The ambition is to provide one framework for the places where modern physics usually fragments into separate stories.

Observer patches

Replace objective reality with overlapping local descriptions that must agree where they intersect.

Emergent spacetime

Use entanglement and holography to recover geometry, causal structure, and gravity.

Quantum mechanics

Treat measurement, overlap, and consistency as the reason quantum behavior looks the way it does.

Gauge structure

Read symmetry and redundancy as consequences of how local patch descriptions are glued together.

Phenomenology

Push toward particle masses, dark matter behavior, and concrete constraints instead of stopping at slogans.

Adversarial testing

Expose the framework to explicit attacks rather than asking for trust, mood affiliation, or mystique.

FAQ

The search terms people actually use.

OPH sits at the intersection of theory-of-everything searches, quantum-gravity searches, and simulation-theory questions. These are the shortest answers to the three most common entry points.

What is OPH?

Observer Patch Holography is a framework where reality is reconstructed from observer-local patches that must agree on overlap.

Is it a theory of everything?

That is the claim: one framework intended to connect quantum mechanics, gravity, gauge structure, and phenomenology from one axiomatic base.

Is this simulation theory?

It intersects with simulation-theory language, but the emphasis is on formal consistency and holographic information flow, not a cartoon external simulator.
