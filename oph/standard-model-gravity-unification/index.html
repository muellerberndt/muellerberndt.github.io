What OPH unifies

OPH uses observer-overlap consistency as the common mechanism. A finite observer has access to a local patch; neighboring patches must agree where their observations overlap. The public world is the fixed point of that consistency problem.

On the gravity side, the framework follows the route from overlap, modular structure, generalized entropy, and horizon thermodynamics toward Lorentz structure and an Einstein branch. On the Standard Model side, it follows compact-gauge reconstruction and realized matter-branch inputs toward the observed quotient structure.

Why it is not just grand unification

A conventional grand unified theory usually tries to embed the Standard Model gauge group into a larger gauge group. OPH's route is different: the gauge group and gravity branch are both downstream of the observer-consistency setup.

That is why this page uses the phrase Standard Model unified with gravity rather than only grand unification. The target is one framework behind spacetime, gravity, gauge structure, particles, records, and observers.
